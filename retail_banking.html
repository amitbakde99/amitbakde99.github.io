Client/Scenario

A mid‑market retail bank sought to improve subscription rates for deposit/credit products while reducing churn. Marketing campaigns were broad and inefficient, with limited personalization and unclear ROI attribution.

Business Problem

  • Low response rates to product campaigns and high acquisition costs.
  • Inability to prioritize high‑propensity customers for cross‑sell/up‑sell.
  • Fragmented view of risk and compliance signals across channels.

Objectives & KPIs

  • Increase campaign conversion rate (CCR) and click‑through rate (CTR).
  • Lift cross‑sell propensity while keeping risk within policy.
  • Reduce churn probability in vulnerable cohorts.
  • Quantify marketing ROI and CAC payback improvements.

Data & Tools

  • Data: Demographics, product holdings, transactions (aggregates), campaign history, channel interactions.
  • Tools: Python (pandas, scikit‑learn, XGBoost), SQL, Tableau/Power BI for dashboards.
  • Governance: basic PII masking; model explainability via SHAP for risk/compliance review.

Methodology

  • Feature engineering: RFM‑style recency/frequency/monetary signals, product tenure, channel affinity, campaign fatigue.
  • Data quality: missing‑value strategy, outlier treatment, target leakage checks.
  • Modeling: baseline logistic regression → random forest → gradient boosting (XGBoost) with Bayesian/Random search.
  • Evaluation: Stratified CV; metrics AUC‑ROC, PR‑AUC, lift@k, calibration (Brier score).
  • Interpretability: global & local SHAP; bias checks on protected attributes (held‑out).
  • Deployment concept: batch scoring for monthly campaigns; API stub for real‑time next‑best‑offer.

Key Findings

  • XGBoost outperformed baselines (AUC +0.07 vs. logistic; PR‑AUC +0.09), well‑calibrated after isotonic calibration.
  • Top drivers: recent digital engagement, multi‑product depth, income proxy features, and relationship tenure.
  • Campaign fatigue penalty and time‑of‑contact windows materially affected conversion.

Business Impact (Pilot)

  • Targeting top decile by propensity yielded ~2.1× conversion vs. business‑as‑usual.
  • Personalized offer sequencing reduced churn risk in a vulnerable cohort by ~12%.
  • Marketing ROI improved via lift‑based audience sizing (fewer wasted impressions).

Business Impact — Executive Summary

  • 2.1× conversion improvement in top‑decile targeting vs BAU.
  • 12% reduction in churn risk for the vulnerable cohort via sequenced offers.
  • Higher ROI by lift‑based audience sizing (fewer wasted impressions); faster CAC payback.

*Illustrative pilot based on historical data; not a real client.

Dashboard & Explainability

  • Executive dashboard: funnel, cohort movement, lift curves, CCR/CPA trends.
  • Risk view: SHAP global summary, top‑k local explanations for adverse decisions.

Next Steps

  • Champion–challenger monitoring; quarterly feature refresh.
  • Uplift modeling for true incremental impact; MTA for multi‑channel attribution.
  • Fairness & drift monitoring aligned to bank policy.
